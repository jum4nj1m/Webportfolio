[OBSERVATION] Throughout my experience as part of a Blue Team, I noticed that blue teaming often feels like a cat-and-mouse game. Users get compromised due to their lack of knowledge, people relying on packages that are no longer maintained, or unpatched/insecure systems leading to compromised. Attacks never truly decrease; they only become more sophisticated, especially with the boom of AI. Alongside that experience, I discovered that I enjoy working on automation (Hey! Not that I’m a slacker), but because I value time more than anything.
[REALIZATION] One day, it occurred to me, why spend all my time just defending the perimeter when I could learn to protect the development process itself?
[MISSION] This is where I decided to focus more on the "Shift-Left" approach and dedicated myself to diving deep into Web Development and DevSecOps to build systems that aren't just functional, but automated and secure by design.
